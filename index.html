UPGRADES / MERGE

The Merge

  • Ethereum is moving to proof-of-stake! The transition, known as The Merge, must first be activated on the Beacon Chain with the Bellatrix upgrade. After this, the proof-of-work chain will migrate to proof-of-stake upon hitting a specific Total Difficulty value.

  • The Bellatrix upgrade is scheduled for epoch 144896 on the Beacon Chain -- 11:34:47am UTC on Sept 6, 2022.

  • The Terminal Total Difficulty value triggering The Merge is 58750000000000000000000, expected between Sept 10-20, 2022.

  • Note: as announced earlier, the Kiln testnet is being sunset. Operators will shut down on September 6, 2022.

Background

Following years of hard work, Ethereum's proof-of-stake upgrade is finally here! The successful upgrade of all public testnets is now complete, and The Merge has been scheduled for the Ethereum mainnet.
The Merge is different from previous network upgrades in two ways. First, node operators need to update both their consensus layer (CL) and execution layer (EL) clients in tandem, rather than just one of the two. Second, the upgrade activates in two phases: the first, named Bellatrix, at an epoch height on the Beacon Chain, and the second, named Paris, upon hitting a Total Difficulty value on the execution layer.

What do I need to do for staking?

As explained above, validators on the Beacon Chain will need to run a post-merge runtime client in addition to their consensus clients. This was highly recommended before the merge, but some validators have outsourced these features to third parties. This was possible because the only data required at the executive level were updates to the deposit contract.

After the merge, validators must ensure that the user transactions and the state transition blocks they create and validate are valid. To do this, each beacon node must be associated with a runtime client. Note that multiple validators can still be associated with the same beacon node and run level client combo. This expands the responsibilities of validators, but also entitles the validator that proposes a block to the associated transaction priority fees (which currently go to miners).

While the validator reward still accumulates on the Beacon Chain and will require a subsequent network upgrade to be withdrawn, the transaction fee will be paid, burned and distributed at the execution level. Validators can specify any Ethereum address as the recipient of the transaction fee.

After updating your consensus client, be sure to set the fee recipient in your validator client settings to ensure that the transaction fee is sent to an address you control. If you have placed a bet using a third party provider, the provider you choose must specify how these fees are distributed. If you have made a deposit on this site, you do not need to do anything else.

Proof of stake

The Beacon Chain upgrade brings proof-of-stake consensus to Ethereum.
For this, we need active participants - known as validators - to propose, verify, and vouch for the validity of blocks. In exchange, honest validators receive financial rewards.
Importantly, as a validator you'll need to post TestnetETH as collateral in other words, have some funds at stake. The only way to become a validator is to make a one-way TestnetETH transaction to the deposit contract on the current Ethereum chain.
I understand proof-of-stake and why validators are needed.

The deposit

To become a validator on the Beacon Chain, you need to deposit 32 TestnetETH per validator that you wish to run.
This is a non-reversible transaction.
Withdrawing your deposit will not be possible until the Shanghai upgrade planned for after the Merge.
I understand that I need to deposit 32 TestnetETH to become a validator, and that the transfer of TestnetETH to the Beacon Chain is one-way, and non-reversible.

Key management

To become a validator you'll need to know about managing keys and protecting a mnemonic. If you are not yet familiar with keys and mnemomics, please do not proceed. Note that during this process you will not derive the keys needed to withdraw later, so store your mnemonic safely in order to be able to withdraw later.
We'll help you create a signing key for every validator you want to run. Because there are no withdrawals until the Shanghai upgrade planned to follow the Merge, you will not create your withdrawal keys now. When it is possible to withdraw your funds, you can derive your withdrawal keys from your mnemonic.
Your mnemonic is the backup for your signing keys and will be the ONLY way to withdraw your TestnetETH when the time comes and no one can help you recover your mnemonic if you lose it.
I understand that keys are my responsibility and that my mnemonic (seed) will be the ONLY WAY to withdraw my funds.

commitment
